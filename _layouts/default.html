Sashank Srinivasan

I am a PhD student in the Mechanical Engineering department at the University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign, part of the Computational Kinetics Group. My PhD advisor is Kelly Stephani.

My research interests are at the intersection of Flow physics (Fluid Mechanics, Heat Transfer, Thermodynamics), Computation (high performance computing), and Applied Mathematics(high-order numerical techniques). I also have interests in Machine Learning and Quantitave Finance.

My PhD research focuses on designing spacecraft thermal protection systems (TPS) using transpiration cooling principles. This is a deviation from the traditional ablative type TPS which lose material due to ablation as a result of extreme heating during atmospheric re-entry.

I have an Bachelors degree in Mechanical Engineeing from BITS-Pilani, Hyderabad Campus, and a MS in Aeronautics and Astronautics from Purdue University where I was a research assistant for Prof. Jonathan Poggie and Prof. Xiangxiong Zhang Poggie.

My Master'S thesis focussed on computational plasma modeling, where I developed 1-D and 2-D solvers for predicting eclectric glow discharges using Discontinuous Galerkin Finite Elememnt Methods. I designed and implemented a novel DG-FEM scheme that preseraves positivity of solution, something which is of physical importance when predicting charge densities.
